# Insomnia – Quick Overview

| Aspect | Key Points | Sources |
|--------|------------|---------|
| **Definition** | A sleep-wake disorder characterized by difficulty **falling asleep**, staying asleep, or obtaining restorative sleep despite adequate opportunity. | [Mayo Clinic](https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/insomnia/symptoms-causes/syc-20355167) |
| **Types** | • **Acute (short-term)** – lasts days to weeks, often linked to stress or life events.<br>• **Chronic** – >=3 nights/week for >=3 months. | [Mayo Clinic](https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/insomnia/symptoms-causes/syc-20355167) |
| **Epidemiology** | Affects ~10-30% of adults worldwide; women and older adults have higher prevalence. | General medical literature (e.g., StatPearls) |
| **Common Causes** | • **Psychological:** stress, anxiety, depression.<br>• **Medical:** chronic pain, asthma, GERD, hyperthyroidism, neurodegenerative disease.<br>• **Pharmacologic:** stimulants, corticosteroids, some antihistamines.<br>• **Lifestyle:** irregular sleep schedule, caffeine/alcohol use, excessive screen time, daytime napping.<br>• **Environmental:** noise, light, uncomfortable bedding. | [Cleveland Clinic](https://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/diseases/12119-insomnia) |
| **Symptoms** | • Difficulty initiating sleep (>=30 min to fall asleep).<br>• Frequent awakenings or early-morning awakenings.<br>• Non-restorative or poor-quality sleep.<br>• Daytime fatigue, irritability, impaired concentration, mood disturbances.<br>• May lead to accidents, reduced quality of life. | [Mayo Clinic](https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/insomnia/symptoms-causes/syc-20355167) |
| **Diagnostic Approach** | 1. **Clinical interview** – sleep history, habits, medical/psychiatric review.<br>2. **Screening tools** – Insomnia Severity Index (ISI), Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index (PSQI).<br>3. **Sleep diary** (2-4 weeks) to track patterns.<br>4. **Rule-out** other sleep disorders (e.g., sleep apnea, restless legs) via polysomnography if indicated. | Standard sleep-medicine guidelines |
| **First-Line Treatment** | **Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia (CBT-I)** – most effective long-term; includes sleep hygiene, stimulus control, sleep restriction, cognitive restructuring, relaxation techniques. | [Cleveland Clinic](https://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/diseases/12119-insomnia) |
| **Pharmacologic Options** (short-term or refractory cases) | • **Benzodiazepine receptor agonists** – zolpidem, eszopiclone.<br>• **Sedating antihistamines** – diphenhydramine (OTC, limited efficacy).<br>• **Melatonin or ramelteon** – chronobiotic agents.<br>• **Low-dose doxepin** – antihistaminic effect.<br>• **Prescription antidepressants** (e.g., trazodone) – used off-label. | [Mayo Clinic](https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/insomnia/diagnosis-treatment/drc-20355173) |
| **Lifestyle / Sleep-Hygiene Recommendations** | • Keep a regular bedtime/wake-time schedule.<br>• Limit caffeine/alcohol 4-6 h before bedtime.<br>• Create a dark, quiet, cool bedroom.<br>• Reserve bed for sleep (no work/electronics).<br>• Avoid long naps; if needed, limit to <30 min early afternoon. | [Cleveland Clinic](https://my.clevelandclinic.org/health/diseases/12119-insomnia) |
| **Potential Complications** | • Daytime impairment (work, school, driving).<br>• Mood disorders (depression, anxiety).<br>• Increased risk of accidents, cardiovascular disease, metabolic dysfunction, impaired immune response. | General medical literature |
| **When to Seek Professional Help** | • Symptoms >3 months (chronic).<br>• Daytime dysfunction affecting safety (e.g., driving).<br>• Suspected underlying medical/psychiatric condition.<br>• Use of sleep-aiding medication >4 weeks without physician guidance. | Standard clinical practice |

---

### Take-away

- **Insomnia** is common and can be acute or chronic.
- **CBT-I** is the gold-standard, non-pharmacologic treatment and should be tried first.
- Medications are useful for short-term relief or when CBT-I isn’t fully effective, but they carry risk of dependence and side-effects.
- Addressing **sleep hygiene**, lifestyle habits, and underlying medical/psychological causes is essential for lasting improvement.
