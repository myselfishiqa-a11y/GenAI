# India - Quick Overview

**Geography** - South Asian sub-continent, 3.28 million km^2 (7th largest). Bordered by Pakistan, China, Nepal, Bhutan, Bangladesh, Myanmar; coastlines on the Indian Ocean, Arabian Sea and Bay of Bengal. Capital: New Delhi; largest city: Mumbai.

**Population** - Approx. 1.48 billion (2026 estimate), the world's most populous nation and the largest democracy.

**Government** - Federal parliamentary republic. President (head of state) and Prime Minister (head of government). Bicameral Parliament (Rajya Sabha & Lok Sabha).

**History Highlights**
- Early human presence ~55 kya; Indus Valley Civilization (c. 2500-1900 BCE).
- Vedic period, emergence of Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism (c. 1500-500 BCE).
- Maurya (Ashoka) and Gupta empires (ancient golden ages).
- Islamic Sultanates and Mughal Empire (13th-18th c.), notable for cultural synthesis and architecture.
- British East India Company rule -> British Crown (1858-1947).
- Independence & partition (1947); Republic established (1950).

**Economy** - Mixed economy, 6th largest nominal GDP (~$4.15 trillion, 2026) and 3rd in PPP (~$18.9 trillion). Key sectors: services (IT & BPO), manufacturing, agriculture. Rapid growth, expanding middle class, but persistent inequality (Gini ~ 25.5).

**Society** - 424 living languages; official language Hindi and English; multilingual, multi-ethnic, and religiously diverse (Hinduism 79.8%, Islam 14.2%, Christianity, Sikhism, etc.). Literacy ~74% (2023). Challenges: gender inequality, child malnutrition, air pollution, regional disparities.

**International Role** - Nuclear-weapon state, major contributor to UN peacekeeping, member of G20, BRICS, and South-South cooperation. Ongoing border disputes with Pakistan (Kashmir) and China.
